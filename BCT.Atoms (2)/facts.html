Facts You Need to Know

Scholarships & Discounts

  • Academics: Students excelling in academics may apply for scholarships. High school honor students are granted scholarships or discounts based on their school classification and size.
  • Athletics: School athletes may be eligible for discounts.
  • Family Discounts: Siblings enrolled simultaneously receive a 10% discount each.
  • Dependents of Classroom Teachers: Children of teachers (public or private) are granted a 10% discount.

Objectives

  • Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering: The course intends to produce competent personnel with proper knowledge and skills in computer hardware and software engineering. With their training, they will be able to cope with advancements in technology and modern trends in computers.
  • Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering: The course intends to produce Industrial Engineers who are equipped with the knowledge and skills in designing, improving, and installing industrial systems. They will be able to blend arts, sciences, and engineering.
  • Bachelor of Science in Information Technology: The course is geared to keep pace with the fast trend in computers and in network communications.
  • Bachelor of Science in Industrial Technology: This course is geared to develop highly technically skilled workers and future industrial managers.
  • Bachelor of Science in Industrial Education: To develop responsible vocational teachers, the course integrates moral values in order to develop well-rounded youth who are well-bred, technically and globally oriented workers and teachers.
  • Associate Courses - 2 Years: The course is leveled to the pursuit of capable industrial workers. It is designed to provide analytical thinking and planning. It is also geared towards the interpretation and evaluation of technical programs. To meet the manpower demands of industry, the courses provide skills in installation, troubleshooting, and maintenance of equipment. In the process, it stimulates students to engage in entrepreneurship.
